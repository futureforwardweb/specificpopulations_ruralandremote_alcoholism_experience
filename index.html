What could have been different
The wake
Nobody saw last Friday from where Jake was standing.
His mum sat at her kitchen table at one in the morning, tea going cold, checking her phone. Liam shared Jake's post and sent it to his best mates Dusty and Mitch like it was something to aspire to.

Three weeks ago
He'd tried once.
Three Fridays back, he sat in the ute for eleven minutes with the engine running. Typed "nah not tonight lads" into the group chat. Read it back. Deleted it. Drove to the pub.
He couldn't find the words that wouldn't make him sound soft. So he went. And here he is again - Saturday morning, staring at the ceiling, $106 lighter, with nothing to show for it but a headache and a feeling he can't name.

Skill One
Assertiveness
Saying what you need. Clearly. Calmly. Without the novel. Short and direct closes the door - over-explaining opens it.
"Not the pub but down for a drive tomorrow." Rejecting the drinking, not the mates. Real ones feel the difference.

Thursday · 6:14 PM · Ute parked on the verge. Engine off.

He pulled over. Engine off. The urge was there — the pub, the noise. Instead he tried a breathing exercise he'd read about at 2am.

Four in. Hold. Four out. Felt stupid the first time. Third cycle, something loosened.

8:30 PM
Before Davo's next shout.

He says nah. Drops his glass. Walks out.
Drives straight home.
No Maccas. No scrolling.

Nobody notices. That's what Jake tells himself. That's the version that makes it easier — the idea that these people don't see him clearly enough to spot the difference. It's protection. If nobody's watching, then nobody can ask the question he doesn't have an answer to yet.

He does it the next Friday.
And the one after that.
He doesn't call it anything.
He just does it.
Three weeks later. Sunday. 7:22 PM.
His phone lit up.

Sunday · 7:22 PM

The same Jake who couldn't say no three months ago, quietly saying it every Friday without a word. Tonight he stopped just watching.
